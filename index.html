Nick Mathews

I'm a graduate student at Oregon State University, earning an M.S. in geotechnical engineering. My research interests are slope stability, geohazards, and mapping. My hobbies include: skiing, climbing, mountain biking, and visual arts.

O-HELP (2017-current)

The Oregon Hazard Explorer for Lifelines Program was started in 2013 by students and faculty in the Geomatics Department, at Oregon State University. This project, which I inherited in 2017, allows users to explore geohazards within the context of a Cascadia Subduction Zone earthquake.

Visit Project Close

Sea Level Rise Migration Project (2017)

Geovisualization of projected human migration in America, due to 1.8 meters of sea level rise.

Visit Project Close

Stomping Grounds (2017)

An autobiographical storymap that visits some of my favorite places, and their outdoor playgrounds.

GUI: Bishop's Simplified Method of Slices (2015)

This GUI (graphical user interface) performs a slope stability analysis using Bishop's Method. Slope geometry, soil properties, and analysis parameters are defined by user.
